7 Reasons Your Resume Is Getting Rejected by ATS in India (2025)

Having reviewed 10,000+ resumes across India and the UAE, Neeraj Singh Bora consistently sees the same mistakes that cause qualified professionals to get filtered out automatically:

1
Using Tables, Columns, and Text Boxes
Most ATS systems — including those used by TCS, Infosys, and MNCs — cannot read multi-column layouts, tables, or text boxes. They scramble the content, making your resume appear incomplete or unreadable to the parser.
2
Missing Keywords From the Job Description
ATS ranks resumes by keyword match. If a job description says "SAP", "Six Sigma", or "digital marketing" and your resume doesn't use the exact same words — even if you have the experience — you score low. This is the #1 reason skilled professionals get filtered out.
3
Non-Standard Section Headings
ATS looks for "Work Experience", "Education", and "Skills" — not "My Journey", "Career Milestones", or "Where I've Been". Creative headings confuse parsers and cause your most important sections to be missed entirely.
4
Scanned PDFs or Image-Based Resumes
A scanned resume is just an image — ATS cannot extract any text from it. On Naukri, this means your profile shows zero skills and zero experience in the database, making you invisible to all recruiter searches.
5
No Quantified Achievements
ATS and recruiters both score higher on resumes with measurable results. "Increased sales by 32%" beats "responsible for sales" every time. Numbers signal impact, making your profile rank higher in both automated and human review.
6
Resume Too Short or Too Long
Under 300 words, and ATS flags your resume as thin with insufficient information. Over 900 words, and some systems truncate your content. The ideal range for most Indian job applications is 400–700 words for experienced professionals.
7
Missing LinkedIn URL or Contact Details
ATS systems and recruiters validate candidates by cross-referencing LinkedIn profiles. A missing LinkedIn URL, incorrect phone format, or unprofessional email address reduces your credibility score and interview callbacks.

How to Improve Your ATS Resume Score — Step-by-Step Guide (2025)

Once you've run our free ATS checker and identified your issues, here's exactly how to fix them. These are the same techniques Neeraj Singh Bora uses when rewriting resumes for our ₹499 service:

1
Use a Single-Column, Text-Based Format

Export your resume as a clean single-column PDF or DOCX. Remove all tables, graphics, columns, and text boxes. Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. Our 11 ATS-optimized resume templates are pre-built to pass all major ATS systems — no formatting work required.

2
Match Keywords From the Job Description

Copy the job description and identify 10–15 key skills and phrases. Naturally integrate these keywords into your skills section, work experience bullets, and summary. If the JD says "digital marketing strategy" — use that exact phrase, not "online marketing planning".

3
Write Strong Bullet Points With Metrics

Every bullet point should follow the formula: Action Verb + Task + Result. Example: "Led a team of 8 engineers to deliver a ₹2.4 crore infrastructure project 3 weeks ahead of schedule." Quantified bullets rank significantly higher in ATS scoring algorithms.

4
Use Standard Section Headings

Use exactly these headings — "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Professional Summary", "Certifications". ATS systems are programmed to recognize these standard labels. Creative alternatives like "My Story" or "Competencies" are often missed by parsers entirely.

5
Keep It Between 400–700 Words

For most Indian professionals with 3–10 years of experience, one page (400–550 words) is ideal. Senior professionals with 10+ years can use two pages (600–750 words). Keep your most recent and relevant experience in the top half — many ATS systems weight the beginning of the document more heavily.
