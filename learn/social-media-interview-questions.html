🟢 Social Media Fundamentals

Q1. What is social media marketing and how does it drive business results?
Beginner▾

Social media marketing uses social platforms (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube, Twitter/X) to reach and engage target audiences, build brand awareness, and drive business objectives like leads, sales, or brand loyalty.
Organic SMM: Free content, community management, brand building
Paid SMM: Paid ads on Meta, LinkedIn, YouTube to drive specific results
Key metrics: Reach, Engagement rate, Follower growth, Click-through rate, Cost per result

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Zomato's social media marketing is a masterclass. Their Instagram and Twitter/X content consistently goes viral with witty, India-specific humor. When IPL season starts, they create cricket-food crossover content. Organic reach: millions. Brand sentiment: extremely positive. Result: brand recall that makes Zomato the first name that comes to mind for food delivery — pure social media marketing impact.
Q2. What is engagement rate and what is a good engagement rate in India?
Beginner▾

Engagement Rate = (Total engagements ÷ Total reach or followers) × 100
Where engagements = likes + comments + shares + saves
Benchmarks for India (2026):
Instagram: 1-3% is average, 3-6% is good, 6%+ is excellent
Facebook: 0.5-1% is average, 1-3% is good
LinkedIn: 2-5% is good
YouTube: 2-4% is good
Note: Nano/micro-influencers (1K-50K followers) typically get higher engagement rates than mega-influencers.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: A food brand with 100,000 Instagram followers posts a Diwali recipe video. It gets 8,000 likes, 500 comments, 2,000 shares, 1,500 saves = 12,000 total engagements. Engagement rate = 12%. This is exceptional and suggests highly relevant content for this audience. The brand should create more recipe content based on this signal.
Q3. What is the difference between reach and impressions?
Beginner▾

Reach — Number of UNIQUE people who saw your content. Each person counted once regardless of how many times they saw it.
Impressions — Total number of times your content was DISPLAYED, including multiple views by the same person.
Impressions ≥ Reach always.
Frequency = Impressions ÷ Reach (average times each person saw your content)

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: A Myntra ad campaign: Reach = 500,000 people. Impressions = 1,500,000. Frequency = 3 (each person saw the ad an average of 3 times). A frequency of 3 is healthy for brand recall. If frequency hits 7-8, ads get fatigued — people start ignoring or hiding them. Time to refresh creative.

🟡 Strategy & Tools

Q4. How do you create a social media content calendar?
Intermediate▾

Social Media Content Calendar framework:
1. Define content pillars (3-5 themes aligned with brand objectives)
2. Determine posting frequency per platform
3. Plan content types (video, carousel, static, story, reel)
4. Mark key dates (festivals, sales, industry events, national days)
5. Balance ratio: 60% value/educational + 30% engagement + 10% promotional
Tools: Buffer, Hootsuite, Meta Business Suite (free), Notion, Google Sheets

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: A fashion brand's content pillars:
1. Style tips (educational) — 3x/week
2. Customer features/UGC (social proof) — 2x/week
3. Product showcases (promotional) — 2x/week
4. Trending content (viral/engagement) — 1x/week
Special dates: Diwali collection launch, Republic Day sale, Valentine's Day, Women's Day. This gives the social media manager a clear, achievable weekly plan rather than scrambling for content daily.
