50 Google Ads Interview Questions
& Answers for 2026

Written by Neeraj Singh Bora — 11+ years in Google Ads & PPC | Google certified in Search, Display, Video, Measurement & GA4

🟢 Beginner (Q1–15) 🟡 Intermediate (Q16–35) 🔴 Advanced (Q36–50)

🟢 Beginner Level — Google Ads Fundamentals

Q1. What is Google Ads and how does it work?
Beginner▾

Google Ads is an online advertising platform where businesses pay to show ads on Google Search, YouTube, Gmail, and millions of partner websites. It works on an auction system — advertisers bid on keywords and Google decides which ads to show based on Ad Rank.

Ad Rank is calculated using: Bid × Quality Score × Expected ad extension impact × Auction-time signals (device, location, time of day).

India Example: Zomato bids on "food delivery near me." When a user searches this on Google, an auction runs in milliseconds. Zomato's ad appears if its Ad Rank is high enough — combining its bid, Quality Score, and relevance.
Q2. What is Ad Rank and what factors affect it?
Beginner▾

Ad Rank determines your ad's position in search results. Higher Ad Rank = better position. The key factors are:

1. Bid amount — Maximum you're willing to pay per click
2. Quality Score — Expected CTR + Ad Relevance + Landing Page Experience
3. Expected impact of ad extensions — Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets
4. Auction-time context — Device, location, time, search query

Key insight: You can beat a competitor with a higher bid if your Quality Score is better. A Naukri.com ad with Quality Score 9 can outrank a competitor with Quality Score 5, even at a lower bid.
Q3. What is Quality Score? What are its 3 components?
Beginner▾

Quality Score is a 1-10 rating Google assigns to each keyword based on three factors:

1. Expected Click-Through Rate (CTR) — How likely users are to click your ad when it appears
2. Ad Relevance — How closely your ad copy matches the user's search intent
3. Landing Page Experience — How relevant, fast, and useful your landing page is for the user

Higher Quality Score = lower CPC + better ad position. A QS of 8-10 can reduce your CPC by up to 50%.

India Example: MakeMyTrip bids on "flights to Goa." If their ad copy says "Book Goa Flights" and the landing page shows Goa flight options, QS will be 8-10. If the landing page shows the homepage instead, QS drops to 4-5.
Q4. What is the difference between CPC, CPM, and CPA?
Beginner▾

CPC (Cost Per Click) — You pay each time someone clicks your ad. Used in Search campaigns. Best for lead generation.
CPM (Cost Per Mille) — You pay per 1,000 impressions. Used in Display and YouTube. Best for awareness.
CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) — Target cost you want to pay per conversion. Google's AI optimizes to hit this target.

India Example: HDFC Bank runs Search ads at CPC of ₹120/click for "home loan India." They run Display ads at CPM of ₹80 for brand awareness. Once they have conversion data, they switch to Target CPA of ₹800 per loan application.
Q5. Explain keyword match types — Broad, Phrase, and Exact.
Beginner▾

Broad Match — Shows ads for related searches, synonyms, and variations. Widest reach, least control.
Phrase Match — Shows ads for searches containing your keyword in order, with words before or after. Added using "quotes".
Exact Match — Shows ads only for searches matching your keyword closely. Added using [brackets].

India Example for keyword "resume writing service":
Broad Match → also shows for: "CV help", "how to write a resume"
Phrase Match → shows for: "affordable resume writing service India", "resume writing service Noida"
Exact Match → shows only for: "resume writing service" or very close variants
Q6. What are negative keywords and why are they important?
Beginner▾

Negative keywords are words or phrases that prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches. They save budget by filtering out low-quality traffic and improve conversion rates by ensuring ads reach only relevant users.

Types: Broad negative, Phrase negative, Exact negative — same logic as regular match types but in reverse.

India Example: Naukri.com running ads for "job search platform" would add negatives like: "free", "student", "internship" — to avoid showing ads to irrelevant users looking for free or student jobs, saving significant budget.
Q7. What is a Responsive Search Ad (RSA)?
Beginner▾

Responsive Search Ads allow you to provide up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions. Google's AI automatically tests different combinations and shows the best-performing combination to each user based on their search query.

Best practice: Write all 15 unique headlines with different angles — include keywords, benefits, CTAs, and USPs. Aim for "Excellent" ad strength rating.

India Example: Myntra runs RSA for "buy shoes online." Headlines include: "Shop 1000+ Shoe Brands", "Free Delivery on Orders ₹499+", "Easy 14-Day Returns", "Latest Nike Adidas Puma Shoes". Google shows the best combination for each user's search.
Q8. What are Ad Extensions (Assets)? Name 5 types.
Beginner▾

Ad Extensions (now called Assets) are additional pieces of information added to your ads that increase visibility and CTR without extra cost. They can improve Ad Rank and provide more reasons to click.

5 key types:
1. Sitelinks — Additional page links below the ad
2. Callouts — Short phrases like "Free Delivery", "24/7 Support"
3. Structured Snippets — Lists of products/services/features
4. Call Extensions — Phone number shown in ad
5. Lead Form Extensions — Capture leads directly from the SERP

India Example: Swiggy's Search ad with sitelinks ("Order Biryani", "Pizza Near Me", "₹50 Off First Order") and callout ("30 Min Delivery", "1000+ Restaurants") gets significantly higher CTR than a plain text ad.
Q9. What is CTR and what is considered a good CTR for Search ads?
Beginner▾

CTR (Click-Through Rate) = (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100. It measures what percentage of people who saw your ad actually clicked on it.

Benchmarks:
Search Ads: 3-5% is average, 8%+ is excellent
Display Ads: 0.1-0.3% is average
YouTube Ads: 0.3-0.7% is average

Low CTR indicates ad copy is not compelling enough or keywords are too broad. High CTR with low conversions means the landing page needs improvement.

India Example: An HDFC Bank ad for "home loan Noida" with CTR of 1% is underperforming. Rewriting headlines to "Get Home Loan Approval in 24 Hours | Apply Now" typically increases CTR to 4-6% in the Indian market.
Q10. What is conversion tracking and why is it important?
Beginner▾

Conversion tracking measures actions that happen after a user clicks your ad — form submissions, purchases, calls, app downloads. It is critical because without conversion tracking, you cannot measure ROI, optimize bids, or use Smart Bidding strategies.

Setup: Google Ads conversion tag (via GTM) fires on the confirmation/thank-you page. The tag sends conversion data back to Google Ads.

India Example: OYO runs Google Search ads for "hotel booking Mumbai." Without conversion tracking, they know who clicked but not who actually booked. With tracking, they know each campaign's true CPA and can optimize accordingly. Without it, budget optimization is guesswork.

🟡 Intermediate Level — Campaigns & Strategy

Q11. What are the different types of Google Ads campaigns?
Intermediate▾

Search — Text ads on Google Search. Best for lead generation and high-intent buyers.
Display — Banner ads on 3M+ websites. Best for remarketing and brand awareness.
Video — YouTube ads. Best for brand awareness and consideration.
Shopping — Product listing ads. Best for e-commerce with product feed.
Performance Max (PMax) — AI-powered across all channels. Best for scaling after 50+ conversions/month.
Demand Gen — Native ads on YouTube feed, Gmail, Discover. Best for mid-funnel consideration.
App — Drive app installs and in-app actions.

India Example: Flipkart uses Shopping ads for product discovery, Search ads for branded terms like "Flipkart electronics sale", Display for remarketing to cart abandoners, and YouTube for festive sale awareness campaigns.
Q12. What is Performance Max (PMax)? When should you use it?
Intermediate▾

Performance Max is Google's AI-powered campaign type that runs across all Google channels — Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, and Discover — simultaneously. Google's AI decides targeting, bidding, and creative combinations automatically.

Use PMax when: 50+ conversions/month, e-commerce with product feed, large budget (₹1.5L+/month), existing Search campaigns with conversion history.

Do NOT use PMax when: New account with no data, under 30 conversions/month, service business with very low lead volume.

India Example: Myntra uses PMax during Big Fashion Festival. AI simultaneously shows Shopping ads, YouTube ads, Gmail ads to the same high-intent buyer at different touchpoints — maximizing conversion probability. For a small boutique with 10 sales/month, PMax would waste budget.
Q13. What is Smart Bidding? Name 4 Smart Bidding strategies.
Intermediate▾

Smart Bidding uses Google's machine learning to automatically optimize bids for each auction based on signals like device, location, time, search query, and user behavior. It requires sufficient conversion data to work effectively.

4 Smart Bidding strategies:
1. Maximize Conversions — Get maximum conversions within budget. No CPA target.
2. Target CPA — Optimize for a specific cost per conversion. Needs 30+ conversions/month.
3. Maximize Conversion Value — Maximize total revenue within budget.
4. Target ROAS — Optimize for a specific return on ad spend. Needs purchase value tracking.

India Example: Amazon India uses Target ROAS of 500% — for every ₹100 spent on ads, they want ₹500 in revenue. Google's AI bidding adjusts bids for each auction to hit this target. During Diwali sale, ROAS might go to 800% due to higher purchase intent.
Q14. What is the correct bidding progression for a new Google Ads campaign?
Intermediate▾

Never start a new campaign with Target CPA — the AI needs conversion data first.

Correct progression:
Day 1–14: Maximize Clicks with Max CPC cap — collect traffic and click data
Day 15–30: Continue Maximize Clicks, monitor which keywords convert
After 30 conversions: Switch to Maximize Conversions
After 50-60 conversions: Switch to Target CPA (set = average CPL × 1.2)

Why CPC cap matters: Without a cap, Google can spend your entire daily budget on 1-2 expensive clicks. CPC cap ensures budget distributes properly and attracts higher-quality traffic.

India Example: A coaching institute launches Google Ads for "UPSC preparation course." Budget: ₹1,000/day. Start: Maximize Clicks with Max CPC cap of ₹80. After 45 days and 35 form submissions, switch to Target CPA of ₹600 (avg CPL was ₹500).
Q15. What is remarketing in Google Ads? How does it work?
Intermediate▾

Remarketing shows ads to people who have previously visited your website or interacted with your brand. It works by placing a cookie on the user's browser via the Google Ads tag. When they browse other websites on the Display Network, your ads follow them.

Types of remarketing audiences:
Standard remarketing — all website visitors
Dynamic remarketing — shows specific products/services users viewed
Customer match — upload customer email list
Similar audiences — reach users similar to your existing customers

India Example: You browse a Sony TV on Flipkart but don't buy. For the next 30 days, you see Flipkart banner ads for that exact Sony TV on news websites, cricket apps, and YouTube. That's dynamic remarketing — highly personalized and effective for recapturing intent.
Q16. What is the Google Ads auction and how does it run?
Intermediate▾

Every Google Search triggers a real-time auction that runs in milliseconds. Here's the process:

1. User searches a keyword
2. Google identifies all advertisers bidding on relevant keywords
3. Ad Rank is calculated for each advertiser (Bid × QS × extensions + context)
4. Advertisers are ranked by Ad Rank
5. Ads are shown in order of Ad Rank
6. Actual CPC = (Ad Rank of advertiser below ÷ Your Quality Score) + ₹0.01

This means you never pay your full bid — you pay just enough to beat the advertiser below you.

India Example: 5 banks bid on "home loan Delhi." The auction runs 10,000 times per day. Each time, the order can change based on device, time, and user signals. HDFC with QS 9 can appear above SBI with a higher bid but QS of 5.
Q17. What is Google Tag Manager (GTM) and how does it help Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

GTM is a tag management system that lets you add, edit, and manage tracking codes (tags) on your website without touching the source code. For Google Ads, GTM is used to deploy conversion tracking tags, remarketing tags, and GA4 event tracking.

Key GTM concepts:
Tags — the tracking code (Google Ads conversion tag)
Triggers — when the tag fires (form submission, button click, page view)
Variables — dynamic values passed with the tag (order value, user ID)

India Example: PolicyBazaar's developer doesn't need to touch the website code every time marketing wants a new tracking pixel. The GTM container is already installed once. Marketing team fires a new trigger: "Tag fires when user reaches /insurance-purchased confirmation page" — conversion tracked in 10 minutes with no dev work.
Q18. What is the Search Impression Share and why does it matter?
Intermediate▾

Search Impression Share = Impressions received ÷ Total impressions eligible. It shows what percentage of eligible searches your ad actually appeared for.

Key metrics:
IS Lost to Budget — percentage you're missing due to insufficient budget
IS Lost to Rank — percentage you're missing due to low Ad Rank
Top IS — how often your ad shows in the top positions

If IS Lost to Budget is high → increase budget. If IS Lost to Rank is high → improve QS or increase bids.

India Example: Byju's has 45% impression share for "online tuition classes." They're losing 35% to budget and 20% to rank. Action: Increase budget first (easier win), then improve ad relevance and landing page to reduce rank loss.
Q19. What is the difference between CBO and manual budgeting in Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

In Google Ads, you set budget at the campaign level (unlike Meta's CBO/ABO distinction). Each campaign has its own daily budget. Google can overspend up to 2x your daily budget on high-intent days but averages out to your monthly limit.

For Performance Max, Google controls budget allocation across channels. For manual Search campaigns, your daily budget controls spend directly.

India Example: You set ₹1,000/day for a Zomato restaurant campaign. During a cricket match day (high food order intent), Google might spend ₹1,800 that day. On a slow Monday, it spends ₹600. Monthly total stays within ₹1,000 × 30 = ₹30,000.
Q20. What is audience targeting in Google Ads Display campaigns?
Intermediate▾

Types of audience targeting for Display:
In-Market Audiences — Users actively researching your product category
Affinity Audiences — Users with long-term interests (luxury shoppers, auto enthusiasts)
Remarketing Lists — Users who visited your website
Customer Match — Upload customer email list
Similar Audiences — Users similar to your remarketing lists
Custom Intent — Users who searched specific keywords on Google

India Example: An ed-tech company like Unacademy uses Custom Intent audience (people who searched "IIT JEE preparation" + "NEET study material") for Display campaigns — showing banner ads to users who already demonstrated exam preparation intent on Google Search.

🔴 Advanced Level — Optimization & Strategy

Q21. What is attribution modeling and which model do you recommend?
Advanced▾

Attribution modeling determines which touchpoints in the customer journey get credit for a conversion.

Models:
Last Click — full credit to last touchpoint (most common but oversimplifies)
First Click — full credit to first touchpoint
Linear — equal credit to all touchpoints
Time Decay — more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion
Data-Driven — AI assigns credit based on actual contribution (recommended)

Recommended: Data-Driven Attribution — requires 3,000+ clicks and 300+ conversions in 30 days to activate.

India Example: A customer's journey to buy Myntra shoes: Display ad (awareness) → YouTube ad (consideration) → Search ad "Myntra shoes offer" (conversion). Last-click gives 100% credit to Search. Data-driven splits credit 20%/35%/45% — giving Display and YouTube their fair share, leading to better budget decisions.
Q22. What is the learning phase in Google Ads and how do you manage it?
Advanced▾

The learning phase occurs when Google's Smart Bidding algorithm collects data to optimize performance. It typically lasts 1-2 weeks and requires approximately 30-50 conversion events to complete.

During learning phase: Performance is unstable, CPA may be higher, delivery can be inconsistent. Do NOT make significant changes — it resets the learning phase.

What resets learning: Changing bid strategy, significant budget changes (>20%), major audience changes, pausing/resuming campaigns.

India Example: A real estate developer launches Google Ads with Target CPA. First 7 days, cost per lead is ₹2,500 vs target of ₹1,200. This is normal learning phase behavior. Many marketers panic and change settings — this resets learning and wastes more budget. The correct approach: wait at least 14 days before evaluating performance.
Q23. How do you improve a campaign with high CTR but low conversion rate?
Advanced▾

High CTR + low conversion = disconnect between ad promise and landing page experience.

Diagnostic steps:
1. Check landing page relevance — does it match the ad's promise?
2. Check page speed — slow pages increase bounce rates
3. Check form length — long forms reduce conversions
4. Check mobile experience — most Indian traffic is mobile
5. Review audience quality — broad match may be attracting wrong users
6. Check geographic targeting — are you targeting relevant cities?

India Example: A CA firm runs an ad "Register Your Company in 3 Days." CTR is 8% but only 1% fill the contact form. Investigation reveals the landing page loads in 7 seconds on mobile and shows generic company info — not the promised registration service. Fix: Create a dedicated fast-loading landing page with a simple form. Conversion rate goes to 5%.
Q24. What is Google Ads Audience Signal in Performance Max?
Advanced▾

Audience Signals are suggestions you provide to Performance Max to help Google's AI find the right audience faster. Unlike traditional targeting, these are hints — not restrictions. Google may show ads beyond your audience signals if it finds better opportunities.

What to include in Audience Signals:
Your remarketing lists (existing website visitors)
Customer Match list (existing customers)
In-Market and Affinity segments relevant to your business
Custom Segments (keywords your customers search)

India Example: Swiggy launches PMax for restaurant partners. Audience Signals include: website visitors (remarketing), "In-Market: Food Delivery Apps", Custom Segment (people who searched "food delivery" + "Zomato alternative"). Google starts there but may expand to nearby audiences it identifies as high converters.
Q25. What is GEO and AEO and how does it affect Google Ads strategy in 2026?
Advanced▾

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — Optimizing for AI-generated search answers from Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity.
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — Optimizing content to appear directly in AI-generated answer boxes.

Impact on Google Ads in 2026: As AI Overviews answer queries without clicks, organic CTR is declining. This is increasing the relative importance of paid search for capturing traffic. Advertisers who only relied on SEO are now shifting more budget to Google Ads.

India Example: Someone searches "best credit card India 2026." Google AI Overview shows a comparison table — zero organic clicks. HDFC Bank's Search ad appears below the AI answer and captures the intent click. The winning strategy: GEO for visibility in AI answers + Google Ads for actual traffic capture.
