50 Google Ads Interview Questions
& Answers for 2026

Written by Neeraj Singh Bora — 11+ years in Google Ads & PPC | Google certified in Search, Display, Video, Measurement & GA4

🟢 Beginner (Q1–15) 🟡 Intermediate (Q16–35) 🔴 Advanced (Q36–50)

🟢 Beginner Level — Google Ads Fundamentals

Q1. What is Google Ads and how does it work?
Beginner▾

Google Ads is an online advertising platform where businesses pay to show ads on Google Search, YouTube, Gmail, and millions of partner websites. It works on an auction system — advertisers bid on keywords and Google decides which ads to show based on Ad Rank.

Ad Rank is calculated using: Bid × Quality Score × Expected ad extension impact × Auction-time signals (device, location, time of day).

India Example: Zomato bids on "food delivery near me." When a user searches this on Google, an auction runs in milliseconds. Zomato's ad appears if its Ad Rank is high enough — combining its bid, Quality Score, and relevance.
Q2. What is Ad Rank and what factors affect it?
Beginner▾

Ad Rank determines your ad's position in search results. Higher Ad Rank = better position. The key factors are:

1. Bid amount — Maximum you're willing to pay per click
2. Quality Score — Expected CTR + Ad Relevance + Landing Page Experience
3. Expected impact of ad extensions — Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets
4. Auction-time context — Device, location, time, search query

Key insight: You can beat a competitor with a higher bid if your Quality Score is better. A Naukri.com ad with Quality Score 9 can outrank a competitor with Quality Score 5, even at a lower bid.
Q3. What is Quality Score? What are its 3 components?
Beginner▾

Quality Score is a 1-10 rating Google assigns to each keyword based on three factors:

1. Expected Click-Through Rate (CTR) — How likely users are to click your ad when it appears
2. Ad Relevance — How closely your ad copy matches the user's search intent
3. Landing Page Experience — How relevant, fast, and useful your landing page is for the user

Higher Quality Score = lower CPC + better ad position. A QS of 8-10 can reduce your CPC by up to 50%.

India Example: MakeMyTrip bids on "flights to Goa." If their ad copy says "Book Goa Flights" and the landing page shows Goa flight options, QS will be 8-10. If the landing page shows the homepage instead, QS drops to 4-5.
Q4. What is the difference between CPC, CPM, and CPA?
Beginner▾

CPC (Cost Per Click) — You pay each time someone clicks your ad. Used in Search campaigns. Best for lead generation.
CPM (Cost Per Mille) — You pay per 1,000 impressions. Used in Display and YouTube. Best for awareness.
CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) — Target cost you want to pay per conversion. Google's AI optimizes to hit this target.

India Example: HDFC Bank runs Search ads at CPC of ₹120/click for "home loan India." They run Display ads at CPM of ₹80 for brand awareness. Once they have conversion data, they switch to Target CPA of ₹800 per loan application.
Q5. Explain keyword match types — Broad, Phrase, and Exact.
Beginner▾

Broad Match — Shows ads for related searches, synonyms, and variations. Widest reach, least control.
Phrase Match — Shows ads for searches containing your keyword in order, with words before or after. Added using "quotes".
Exact Match — Shows ads only for searches matching your keyword closely. Added using [brackets].

India Example for keyword "resume writing service":
Broad Match → also shows for: "CV help", "how to write a resume"
Phrase Match → shows for: "affordable resume writing service India", "resume writing service Noida"
Exact Match → shows only for: "resume writing service" or very close variants
Q6. What are negative keywords and why are they important?
Beginner▾

Negative keywords are words or phrases that prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches. They save budget by filtering out low-quality traffic and improve conversion rates by ensuring ads reach only relevant users.

Types: Broad negative, Phrase negative, Exact negative — same logic as regular match types but in reverse.

India Example: Naukri.com running ads for "job search platform" would add negatives like: "free", "student", "internship" — to avoid showing ads to irrelevant users looking for free or student jobs, saving significant budget.
Q7. What is a Responsive Search Ad (RSA)?
Beginner▾

Responsive Search Ads allow you to provide up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions. Google's AI automatically tests different combinations and shows the best-performing combination to each user based on their search query.

Best practice: Write all 15 unique headlines with different angles — include keywords, benefits, CTAs, and USPs. Aim for "Excellent" ad strength rating.

India Example: Myntra runs RSA for "buy shoes online." Headlines include: "Shop 1000+ Shoe Brands", "Free Delivery on Orders ₹499+", "Easy 14-Day Returns", "Latest Nike Adidas Puma Shoes". Google shows the best combination for each user's search.
Q8. What are Ad Extensions (Assets)? Name 5 types.
Beginner▾

Ad Extensions (now called Assets) are additional pieces of information added to your ads that increase visibility and CTR without extra cost. They can improve Ad Rank and provide more reasons to click.

5 key types:
1. Sitelinks — Additional page links below the ad
2. Callouts — Short phrases like "Free Delivery", "24/7 Support"
3. Structured Snippets — Lists of products/services/features
4. Call Extensions — Phone number shown in ad
5. Lead Form Extensions — Capture leads directly from the SERP

India Example: Swiggy's Search ad with sitelinks ("Order Biryani", "Pizza Near Me", "₹50 Off First Order") and callout ("30 Min Delivery", "1000+ Restaurants") gets significantly higher CTR than a plain text ad.
Q9. What is CTR and what is considered a good CTR for Search ads?
Beginner▾

CTR (Click-Through Rate) = (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100. It measures what percentage of people who saw your ad actually clicked on it.

Benchmarks:
Search Ads: 3-5% is average, 8%+ is excellent
Display Ads: 0.1-0.3% is average
YouTube Ads: 0.3-0.7% is average

Low CTR indicates ad copy is not compelling enough or keywords are too broad. High CTR with low conversions means the landing page needs improvement.

India Example: An HDFC Bank ad for "home loan Noida" with CTR of 1% is underperforming. Rewriting headlines to "Get Home Loan Approval in 24 Hours | Apply Now" typically increases CTR to 4-6% in the Indian market.
Q10. What is conversion tracking and why is it important?
Beginner▾

Conversion tracking measures actions that happen after a user clicks your ad — form submissions, purchases, calls, app downloads. It is critical because without conversion tracking, you cannot measure ROI, optimize bids, or use Smart Bidding strategies.

Setup: Google Ads conversion tag (via GTM) fires on the confirmation/thank-you page. The tag sends conversion data back to Google Ads.

India Example: OYO runs Google Search ads for "hotel booking Mumbai." Without conversion tracking, they know who clicked but not who actually booked. With tracking, they know each campaign's true CPA and can optimize accordingly. Without it, budget optimization is guesswork.

🟡 Intermediate Level — Campaigns & Strategy

Q11. What are the different types of Google Ads campaigns?
Intermediate▾

Search — Text ads on Google Search. Best for lead generation and high-intent buyers.
Display — Banner ads on 3M+ websites. Best for remarketing and brand awareness.
Video — YouTube ads. Best for brand awareness and consideration.
Shopping — Product listing ads. Best for e-commerce with product feed.
Performance Max (PMax) — AI-powered across all channels. Best for scaling after 50+ conversions/month.
Demand Gen — Native ads on YouTube feed, Gmail, Discover. Best for mid-funnel consideration.
App — Drive app installs and in-app actions.

India Example: Flipkart uses Shopping ads for product discovery, Search ads for branded terms like "Flipkart electronics sale", Display for remarketing to cart abandoners, and YouTube for festive sale awareness campaigns.
Q12. What is Performance Max (PMax)? When should you use it?
Intermediate▾

Performance Max is Google's AI-powered campaign type that runs across all Google channels — Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, and Discover — simultaneously. Google's AI decides targeting, bidding, and creative combinations automatically.

Use PMax when: 50+ conversions/month, e-commerce with product feed, large budget (₹1.5L+/month), existing Search campaigns with conversion history.

Do NOT use PMax when: New account with no data, under 30 conversions/month, service business with very low lead volume.

India Example: Myntra uses PMax during Big Fashion Festival. AI simultaneously shows Shopping ads, YouTube ads, Gmail ads to the same high-intent buyer at different touchpoints — maximizing conversion probability. For a small boutique with 10 sales/month, PMax would waste budget.
Q13. What is Smart Bidding? Name 4 Smart Bidding strategies.
Intermediate▾

Smart Bidding uses Google's machine learning to automatically optimize bids for each auction based on signals like device, location, time, search query, and user behavior. It requires sufficient conversion data to work effectively.

4 Smart Bidding strategies:
1. Maximize Conversions — Get maximum conversions within budget. No CPA target.
2. Target CPA — Optimize for a specific cost per conversion. Needs 30+ conversions/month.
3. Maximize Conversion Value — Maximize total revenue within budget.
4. Target ROAS — Optimize for a specific return on ad spend. Needs purchase value tracking.

India Example: Amazon India uses Target ROAS of 500% — for every ₹100 spent on ads, they want ₹500 in revenue. Google's AI bidding adjusts bids for each auction to hit this target. During Diwali sale, ROAS might go to 800% due to higher purchase intent.
Q14. What is the correct bidding progression for a new Google Ads campaign?
Intermediate▾

Never start a new campaign with Target CPA — the AI needs conversion data first.

Correct progression:
Day 1–14: Maximize Clicks with Max CPC cap — collect traffic and click data
Day 15–30: Continue Maximize Clicks, monitor which keywords convert
After 30 conversions: Switch to Maximize Conversions
After 50-60 conversions: Switch to Target CPA (set = average CPL × 1.2)

Why CPC cap matters: Without a cap, Google can spend your entire daily budget on 1-2 expensive clicks. CPC cap ensures budget distributes properly and attracts higher-quality traffic.

India Example: A coaching institute launches Google Ads for "UPSC preparation course." Budget: ₹1,000/day. Start: Maximize Clicks with Max CPC cap of ₹80. After 45 days and 35 form submissions, switch to Target CPA of ₹600 (avg CPL was ₹500).
Q15. What is remarketing in Google Ads? How does it work?
Intermediate▾

Remarketing shows ads to people who have previously visited your website or interacted with your brand. It works by placing a cookie on the user's browser via the Google Ads tag. When they browse other websites on the Display Network, your ads follow them.

Types of remarketing audiences:
Standard remarketing — all website visitors
Dynamic remarketing — shows specific products/services users viewed
Customer match — upload customer email list
Similar audiences — reach users similar to your existing customers

India Example: You browse a Sony TV on Flipkart but don't buy. For the next 30 days, you see Flipkart banner ads for that exact Sony TV on news websites, cricket apps, and YouTube. That's dynamic remarketing — highly personalized and effective for recapturing intent.
Q16. What is the Google Ads auction and how does it run?
Intermediate▾

Every Google Search triggers a real-time auction that runs in milliseconds. Here's the process:

1. User searches a keyword
2. Google identifies all advertisers bidding on relevant keywords
3. Ad Rank is calculated for each advertiser (Bid × QS × extensions + context)
4. Advertisers are ranked by Ad Rank
5. Ads are shown in order of Ad Rank
6. Actual CPC = (Ad Rank of advertiser below ÷ Your Quality Score) + ₹0.01

This means you never pay your full bid — you pay just enough to beat the advertiser below you.

India Example: 5 banks bid on "home loan Delhi." The auction runs 10,000 times per day. Each time, the order can change based on device, time, and user signals. HDFC with QS 9 can appear above SBI with a higher bid but QS of 5.
Q17. What is Google Tag Manager (GTM) and how does it help Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

GTM is a tag management system that lets you add, edit, and manage tracking codes (tags) on your website without touching the source code. For Google Ads, GTM is used to deploy conversion tracking tags, remarketing tags, and GA4 event tracking.

Key GTM concepts:
Tags — the tracking code (Google Ads conversion tag)
Triggers — when the tag fires (form submission, button click, page view)
Variables — dynamic values passed with the tag (order value, user ID)

India Example: PolicyBazaar's developer doesn't need to touch the website code every time marketing wants a new tracking pixel. The GTM container is already installed once. Marketing team fires a new trigger: "Tag fires when user reaches /insurance-purchased confirmation page" — conversion tracked in 10 minutes with no dev work.
Q18. What is the Search Impression Share and why does it matter?
Intermediate▾

Search Impression Share = Impressions received ÷ Total impressions eligible. It shows what percentage of eligible searches your ad actually appeared for.

Key metrics:
IS Lost to Budget — percentage you're missing due to insufficient budget
IS Lost to Rank — percentage you're missing due to low Ad Rank
Top IS — how often your ad shows in the top positions

If IS Lost to Budget is high → increase budget. If IS Lost to Rank is high → improve QS or increase bids.

India Example: Byju's has 45% impression share for "online tuition classes." They're losing 35% to budget and 20% to rank. Action: Increase budget first (easier win), then improve ad relevance and landing page to reduce rank loss.
Q19. What is the difference between CBO and manual budgeting in Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

In Google Ads, you set budget at the campaign level (unlike Meta's CBO/ABO distinction). Each campaign has its own daily budget. Google can overspend up to 2x your daily budget on high-intent days but averages out to your monthly limit.

For Performance Max, Google controls budget allocation across channels. For manual Search campaigns, your daily budget controls spend directly.

India Example: You set ₹1,000/day for a Zomato restaurant campaign. During a cricket match day (high food order intent), Google might spend ₹1,800 that day. On a slow Monday, it spends ₹600. Monthly total stays within ₹1,000 × 30 = ₹30,000.
Q20. What is audience targeting in Google Ads Display campaigns?
Intermediate▾

Types of audience targeting for Display:
In-Market Audiences — Users actively researching your product category
Affinity Audiences — Users with long-term interests (luxury shoppers, auto enthusiasts)
Remarketing Lists — Users who visited your website
Customer Match — Upload customer email list
Similar Audiences — Users similar to your remarketing lists
Custom Intent — Users who searched specific keywords on Google

India Example: An ed-tech company like Unacademy uses Custom Intent audience (people who searched "IIT JEE preparation" + "NEET study material") for Display campaigns — showing banner ads to users who already demonstrated exam preparation intent on Google Search.

🔴 Advanced Level — Optimization & Strategy

Q21. What is attribution modeling and which model do you recommend?
Advanced▾

Attribution modeling determines which touchpoints in the customer journey get credit for a conversion.

Models:
Last Click — full credit to last touchpoint (most common but oversimplifies)
First Click — full credit to first touchpoint
Linear — equal credit to all touchpoints
Time Decay — more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion
Data-Driven — AI assigns credit based on actual contribution (recommended)

Recommended: Data-Driven Attribution — requires 3,000+ clicks and 300+ conversions in 30 days to activate.

India Example: A customer's journey to buy Myntra shoes: Display ad (awareness) → YouTube ad (consideration) → Search ad "Myntra shoes offer" (conversion). Last-click gives 100% credit to Search. Data-driven splits credit 20%/35%/45% — giving Display and YouTube their fair share, leading to better budget decisions.
Q22. What is the learning phase in Google Ads and how do you manage it?
Advanced▾

The learning phase occurs when Google's Smart Bidding algorithm collects data to optimize performance. It typically lasts 1-2 weeks and requires approximately 30-50 conversion events to complete.

During learning phase: Performance is unstable, CPA may be higher, delivery can be inconsistent. Do NOT make significant changes — it resets the learning phase.

What resets learning: Changing bid strategy, significant budget changes (>20%), major audience changes, pausing/resuming campaigns.

India Example: A real estate developer launches Google Ads with Target CPA. First 7 days, cost per lead is ₹2,500 vs target of ₹1,200. This is normal learning phase behavior. Many marketers panic and change settings — this resets learning and wastes more budget. The correct approach: wait at least 14 days before evaluating performance.
Q23. How do you improve a campaign with high CTR but low conversion rate?
Advanced▾

High CTR + low conversion = disconnect between ad promise and landing page experience.

Diagnostic steps:
1. Check landing page relevance — does it match the ad's promise?
2. Check page speed — slow pages increase bounce rates
3. Check form length — long forms reduce conversions
4. Check mobile experience — most Indian traffic is mobile
5. Review audience quality — broad match may be attracting wrong users
6. Check geographic targeting — are you targeting relevant cities?

India Example: A CA firm runs an ad "Register Your Company in 3 Days." CTR is 8% but only 1% fill the contact form. Investigation reveals the landing page loads in 7 seconds on mobile and shows generic company info — not the promised registration service. Fix: Create a dedicated fast-loading landing page with a simple form. Conversion rate goes to 5%.
Q24. What is Google Ads Audience Signal in Performance Max?
Advanced▾

Audience Signals are suggestions you provide to Performance Max to help Google's AI find the right audience faster. Unlike traditional targeting, these are hints — not restrictions. Google may show ads beyond your audience signals if it finds better opportunities.

What to include in Audience Signals:
Your remarketing lists (existing website visitors)
Customer Match list (existing customers)
In-Market and Affinity segments relevant to your business
Custom Segments (keywords your customers search)

India Example: Swiggy launches PMax for restaurant partners. Audience Signals include: website visitors (remarketing), "In-Market: Food Delivery Apps", Custom Segment (people who searched "food delivery" + "Zomato alternative"). Google starts there but may expand to nearby audiences it identifies as high converters.
Q25. What is GEO and AEO and how does it affect Google Ads strategy in 2026?
Advanced▾

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — Optimizing for AI-generated search answers from Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity.
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — Optimizing content to appear directly in AI-generated answer boxes.

Impact on Google Ads in 2026: As AI Overviews answer queries without clicks, organic CTR is declining. This is increasing the relative importance of paid search for capturing traffic. Advertisers who only relied on SEO are now shifting more budget to Google Ads.

India Example: Someone searches "best credit card India 2026." Google AI Overview shows a comparison table — zero organic clicks. HDFC Bank's Search ad appears below the AI answer and captures the intent click. The winning strategy: GEO for visibility in AI answers + Google Ads for actual traffic capture.
Q26. What is ROAS and how do you calculate it?
Intermediate▾

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) measures revenue generated for every rupee spent on advertising. Formula: ROAS = Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend × 100. A ROAS of 400% means for every ₹100 spent, you earned ₹400 in revenue.
Difference from ROI: ROAS measures revenue (gross), ROI measures profit (net). You can have positive ROAS but negative ROI if margins are thin.
Target ROAS bidding: Google's Smart Bidding strategy that optimizes bids to hit a specific ROAS target. Requires conversion value tracking and minimum 15–20 purchase conversions/month.

🇮🇳 India Example: Amazon India sets Target ROAS of 600% for electronics campaigns — for every ₹100 spent, they want ₹600 revenue. During Diwali sale, actual ROAS spikes to 1,200% due to high purchase intent. Off-season it drops to 350%. A Target ROAS campaign automatically adjusts bids for each auction to stay near the 600% target over time.
Q27. What is the difference between Search Network and Display Network?
Intermediate▾

Search Network: Text ads shown on Google Search results and search partner sites when users actively search for keywords. High intent — user is looking for your product/service right now.
Display Network: Banner and image ads shown on 3 million+ websites, apps, YouTube and Gmail. Low intent — user is not searching, you are interrupting their browsing.
Use Search for: Lead generation, high-intent buyers, direct response campaigns.
Use Display for: Brand awareness, remarketing, reaching users at top of funnel.
Rule: Never combine Search and Display in one campaign — targeting and bidding strategies are completely different.

🇮🇳 India Example: PolicyBazaar runs Search Network ads for 'best health insurance plan India' (high intent — user is actively comparing plans). They run Display Network ads on finance news websites and cricket apps (brand awareness — showing their name to people who may need insurance in future). Same brand, same budget split 70% Search / 30% Display — two completely separate campaigns.
Q28. What is a landing page and how does it affect Quality Score?
Intermediate▾

A landing page is the web page users arrive at after clicking your ad. It is one of three components of Quality Score. Google evaluates landing pages on:
1. Relevance: Does the landing page content match the ad and the keyword?
2. Originality: Does it provide unique, useful content — not just duplicate content?
3. Transparency: Is there clear business information, contact details, privacy policy?
4. Navigation: Can users easily find what they need?
5. Loading speed: Slow landing pages reduce Quality Score. Target under 3 seconds on mobile.
High landing page experience = better Quality Score = lower CPC = better ad position.

🇮🇳 India Example: Naukri.com runs a Search ad for 'upload resume for jobs.' If the ad links to their homepage (generic), landing page experience is Poor — Quality Score drops. If the ad links directly to '/resume-upload' page that immediately shows the upload form, landing page experience is Good. Same ad, same bid — but dedicated landing page gives Quality Score 8 vs 4 and reduces CPC by 40%.
Q29. What is account structure in Google Ads and why does it matter?
Intermediate▾

Google Ads account structure flows: Account → Campaigns → Ad Groups → Keywords → Ads. Good structure improves Quality Score, budget control and reporting.
Best practice — SKAG vs themed ad groups:
SKAG (Single Keyword Ad Groups): one keyword per ad group — maximum relevance, best Quality Score, harder to manage at scale.
Themed Ad Groups: 5-15 related keywords per group — easier management, slightly lower relevance.
Campaign structure by: Geography (India vs Gulf), device, product/service line, audience, match type.
Why it matters: Poor structure = irrelevant ads = low Quality Score = high CPC = wasted budget.

🇮🇳 India Example: Byju's structures Google Ads campaigns by: Exam type (UPSC, JEE, NEET, CAT), Geography (Tier-1 vs Tier-2 cities), and Device (mobile-only campaign for app installs vs desktop for website signups). This lets them set different bids by exam type (UPSC has higher CPC), control budget per geography, and measure conversion rates separately for app vs web — impossible with a single campaign.
Q30. What is ad scheduling and how do you use it?
Intermediate▾

Ad scheduling (dayparting) controls when your ads show — by day of week and hour of day. You can show ads only during peak conversion times or adjust bids by time period.
Two uses:
1. Show/hide ads at specific times (e.g., only during business hours for B2B)
2. Bid adjustments by time (e.g., +30% on weekday evenings when conversions are highest, -50% on Sundays)
How to set: Campaign settings → Ad schedule → Add time periods and bid adjustments.
Data-driven approach: Run campaigns for 30+ days, then check Conversions by Hour of Day in reports. Increase bids during high-conversion hours, decrease during low-conversion hours.

🇮🇳 India Example: A CA firm in Delhi running Google Ads for 'company registration services' finds from their data that 75% of form submissions happen Monday–Friday 10am–6pm, and almost zero on Sundays. They set ad schedule to: show full time (for impression share data), but apply -90% bid adjustment on Sundays and +20% on weekday mornings 10am–12pm. Result: 30% lower CPA by not wasting budget on low-intent Sunday traffic.
Q31. What is Customer Match in Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

Customer Match lets you upload a list of customer email addresses, phone numbers, or postal addresses to Google Ads. Google matches these to signed-in Google users and lets you target or exclude them in Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail and Shopping campaigns.
Use cases:
1. Target existing customers with upsell campaigns
2. Exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns (avoid paying for them again)
3. Create similar audiences based on your best customers
4. Re-engage lapsed customers with special offers
Requirements: Minimum 1,000 matched users to activate. Match rates typically 30–60% in India (not everyone uses same email for Google).

🇮🇳 India Example: Myntra uploads a list of 500,000 customers who purchased last Diwali but not this year. They target these lapsed customers on Gmail with personalized 'We Miss You — 20% Off' ads. Separately, they exclude this same list from their regular acquisition campaigns (no point paying for returning customers via paid search). Result: ₹40 CPL on Customer Match re-engagement vs ₹120 CPL for new customer acquisition.
Q32. How do you structure a Google Ads audit? What do you check?
Intermediate▾

A Google Ads audit systematically reviews an account to find wasted spend and improvement opportunities. Key areas:
1. Account structure: Campaign/ad group organization, keyword overlap
2. Keywords: Search term reports (irrelevant queries), missing negatives, keyword duplication
3. Quality Score: Below-average QS keywords (below 6), landing page issues
4. Bidding: Is bidding strategy appropriate? Is it in learning phase?
5. Ad copy: RSA ad strength, missing headlines, CTR vs benchmark
6. Conversion tracking: Is it set up correctly? Any double-counting?
7. Extensions/Assets: All available extensions added?
8. Budget: IS Lost to Budget, IS Lost to Rank
9. Attribution: Which model is being used?
10. Audience: Remarketing lists attached? Exclusions in place?

🇮🇳 India Example: Auditing a real estate client's Google Ads account: Found search terms like 'rent apartment Delhi' getting clicks on a 'buy flat Delhi' campaign (wrong intent, wasted ₹8,000/month). Found no negative keywords list at all — added 200 negatives. Found conversion tracking firing on every page load, not just form submission — fixing this showed actual CPA was 3x higher than reported. Three-hour audit saved ₹40,000/month in wasted spend.
Q33. What is Enhanced Conversions in Google Ads?
Intermediate▾

Enhanced Conversions improves conversion tracking accuracy by sending hashed (SHA-256 encrypted) first-party customer data (email, phone, name) to Google when a conversion occurs. Google matches this to signed-in Google users to fill gaps caused by cookie restrictions, iOS privacy changes and browser blocking.
Why it matters in 2026: With third-party cookies declining, standard conversion tags miss 15–30% of conversions. Enhanced Conversions recovers these using privacy-safe first-party data.
Two types:
Enhanced Conversions for Web — website form submissions
Enhanced Conversions for Leads — offline lead import matching
Setup: Via Google Tag or GTM. Requires collecting email/phone on your form.

🇮🇳 India Example: HDFC Bank runs Search Ads for home loans. Standard conversion tracking reports 200 applications/month. After implementing Enhanced Conversions (sending hashed email from form submission), actual reported conversions increase to 260/month — 30% more. The difference: customers who completed the form on iOS Safari (which blocks cookies) are now matched via their Google account email. Better data = better Smart Bidding decisions.
Q34. What is Dynamic Search Ads (DSA) and when should you use it?
Intermediate▾

Dynamic Search Ads automatically generate headlines and landing page URLs based on your website content — you don't manually set keywords. Google crawls your website, matches search queries to your content, and generates the ad headline dynamically.
Best use cases:
1. Large e-commerce sites with thousands of product pages (impossible to keyword-map manually)
2. Finding queries you're missing in regular campaigns
3. Filling keyword gaps when launching new campaigns
Key risk: Google may match irrelevant pages if your website has thin content, unrelated pages, or out-of-stock products. Use page feed or URL filters to exclude.
Not recommended for: New websites, highly regulated industries, limited page inventory.

🇮🇳 India Example: Flipkart has 100 million+ product pages — impossible to create keywords for each. They use DSA with page feed targeting only category pages (/electronics, /fashion, /furniture). When someone searches 'boAt Airdopes 141 best price,' DSA automatically generates headline 'boAt Airdopes 141 — ₹1,299' and links to the exact product page. This captures long-tail queries their keyword campaigns miss. DSA contributes 15-20% of their Search conversions.
Q35. What is Target Impression Share bidding strategy?
Intermediate▾

Target Impression Share is a Smart Bidding strategy that automatically sets bids to achieve a target percentage of impressions at a specific position — Absolute Top (first ad position), Top (above organic), or Anywhere on the page.
Use cases:
1. Brand terms — ensure your brand always shows at Absolute Top
2. Conquesting competitor keywords — dominate their brand searches
3. High-priority products or launches needing maximum visibility
Not for: ROI-focused campaigns — it prioritizes impressions over conversions and can be expensive.
Setting: Choose target location (Absolute Top / Top / Anywhere) and max CPC cap to control spend.

🇮🇳 India Example: Zomato uses Target Impression Share at 90% Absolute Top for their own brand keyword 'Zomato.' This ensures their ad always appears first — critical because competitors bid on 'Zomato' to steal clicks. They accept the high CPC because brand term conversions have the highest ROI. For generic keywords like 'food delivery,' they switch to Target CPA — impression share doesn't matter there, only cost per order.
Q36. How do you reduce Cost Per Lead (CPL) in Google Ads?
Advanced▾

Reducing CPL requires improving both campaign efficiency and conversion rate:
Campaign-level fixes:
1. Add negative keywords — eliminate irrelevant clicks
2. Pause low-performing keywords (high spend, zero conversions)
3. Improve Quality Score — better ad relevance, better landing pages
4. Adjust bid by device, location, time — shift budget to what converts
5. Switch to Target CPA once you have 30+ conversions
Landing page fixes:
6. Reduce form fields — every field you remove increases conversion rate
7. Add trust signals (testimonials, certifications, phone number)
8. Improve page speed — 1 second delay = 7% lower conversion rate
9. Match ad message to landing page exactly
Audience fixes:
10. Add audience bid adjustments for high-converting demographics
11. Exclude audiences that never convert

🇮🇳 India Example: A Noida coaching institute had CPL of ₹2,200 for UPSC courses. Step-by-step reduction: Added 180 negative keywords (irrelevant free resources searchers) → CPL dropped to ₹1,600. Reduced form from 8 fields to 4 fields → CPL dropped to ₹1,100. Added testimonial section and UPSC rank topper photos on landing page → CPL dropped to ₹780. Same budget, same keywords — just optimization. CPL reduced 65% in 3 months.
Q37. What is Consent Mode v2 and why does it matter for Google Ads?
Advanced▾

Consent Mode v2 (released March 2024) is Google's framework that allows advertisers to run Google Ads tags and GA4 in a privacy-compliant manner when users decline cookies. It uses four parameters: analytics_storage, ad_storage, ad_user_data, and ad_personalization.
Why it's critical in 2026:
Without Consent Mode, when a user declines cookies (common with GDPR, CCPA, India's DPDP Act), your conversion tags don't fire — creating data gaps. With Consent Mode v2, Google uses modeled conversions to estimate what those non-consenting users would have done.
Impact: Advertisers without Consent Mode v2 lose 20-40% of their conversion data, which causes Smart Bidding to underperform.
Setup: Implement via GTM, update tag configuration, add CMP (Consent Management Platform).

🇮🇳 India Example: A Bangalore SaaS company gets significant European traffic. Before Consent Mode v2: 40% of EU users decline cookies → conversion data shows 60% of actual conversions → Smart Bidding underbids for EU traffic. After Consent Mode v2: Google models the missing 40% conversions → full picture restored → Target CPA campaign performs 28% better in EU market. As India's DPDP Act enforcement increases in 2026, this will become critical for India too.
Q38. How do you set up conversion tracking for a lead generation website using GTM?
Advanced▾

Step-by-step Google Ads conversion tracking via GTM:
Step 1: In Google Ads → Tools → Conversions → Create new conversion → Website
Step 2: Set: Conversion name (Lead Submission), Category (Lead), Value (if known), Count (One per click for leads), Click-through window (30 days), View-through window (1 day)
Step 3: Enable Conversion Modeling (for privacy gaps)
Step 4: Copy Global Site Tag and Event Snippet from Google Ads
Step 5: In GTM: Create Tag → Google Ads Conversion Tracking → Paste Conversion ID and Label
Step 6: Set trigger: Page View → Page URL contains '/thank-you' (confirmation page)
Critical rule: Tag fires on confirmation page ONLY — not on the form button click. Button click fires before submission succeeds; confirmation page fires only after successful submission.
Step 7: Test with GTM Preview mode. Verify in Google Ads → Conversions shows 'Recording conversions'

🇮🇳 India Example: NeuroRank.ai has a 'Book Free Audit' form. Wrong setup (common mistake): Tag fires on button click — but 30% of users click submit then get a validation error and never complete. This inflates conversions by 30%. Correct setup: Tag fires only when user lands on '/audit-booked-successfully' confirmation page — fires only on genuine form completions. This data difference means Smart Bidding gets accurate signals and optimizes correctly.
Q39. What is the difference between view-through conversion and click-through conversion?
Advanced▾

Click-through conversion: User clicks your ad and then converts within the click conversion window (typically 30 days). This is directly attributable to your ad.
View-through conversion: User sees your Display or YouTube ad (impression, no click) and then converts within the view-through window (typically 1 day) through another channel. Useful for measuring brand awareness impact.
Problem with view-through: Highly inflated — almost anyone who converts after seeing any display impression gets attributed, even if they would have converted anyway. Can double-count conversions.
Best practice: Use view-through window of 1 day maximum. Don't include view-through conversions in your main optimization metric for Smart Bidding — use only click-through conversions.

🇮🇳 India Example: OYO Rooms runs Display ads for brand awareness. Their conversion report shows 1,200 monthly conversions — 400 click-through + 800 view-through. The 800 view-through conversions are mostly people who saw OYO's banner on a cricket website and then booked OYO directly two hours later (they were going to book anyway). If OYO includes these 800 in their Target CPA calculation, CPA looks ₹300 instead of the true ₹900. Decisions made on inflated data = wasted budget.
Q40. How do you manage Google Ads for multiple locations across India?

Option 2 — Location bid adjustments in one campaign: +30% for Delhi, +20% for Mumbai, -40% for Tier-3 cities. Simpler but less granular control.
Option 3 — Performance Max with location targets: Google AI optimizes across all locations automatically.
Key metrics to track per city: CPC (varies hugely — Delhi Search CPCs 2-3x higher than Tier-2 cities), CVR, CPL, volume.
Common India insight: Tier-2 cities (Lucknow, Jaipur, Surat) often have lower CPCs but similar conversion rates — high ROI vs metro cities.

🇮🇳 India Example: ATS Resume Checker's agency runs campaigns for Mumbai (high competition, ₹180 CPC), Delhi (₹150 CPC), Bangalore (₹140 CPC), Hyderabad (₹110 CPC) and Tier-2 cities combined (₹55 CPC). Q42. How do you track offline conversions in Google Ads?
Advanced▾

Offline conversion tracking imports real-world conversions (phone calls that become sales, leads that close in CRM) back into Google Ads. This gives Smart Bidding accurate data about which clicks actually generated revenue.
Setup process:
1. Enable auto-tagging in Google Ads (GCLID parameter appended to URLs)
2. Store GCLID from landing page in your CRM alongside the lead
3. When lead converts offline (meeting, call, sale), note the conversion date
4. Export GCLID + conversion date + value from CRM
5. Upload to Google Ads → Conversions → Upload
Or: Use Zapier/HubSpot/Salesforce integrations for automatic import.
Why critical: Without offline import, Google Ads only sees form fills — not whether those leads became paying customers. Smart Bidding optimizes for real revenue.

🇮🇳 India Example: A Mumbai real estate company runs Google Ads for 'flats for sale in Mumbai.' They get 100 form fills/month (CPA ₹2,000). But only 3 leads close to actual sales (cost per sale ₹66,000). By importing offline conversions (which 3 GCLID leads became sales), Smart Bidding learns which search queries, audiences, and times generate actual buyers — not just form fills. Over 3 months, cost per actual sale drops from ₹66,000 to ₹38,000.

Q44. What is Demand Gen (formerly Discovery Ads) in Google Ads?
Advanced▾

Demand Gen is Google's native advertising format that shows immersive, image and video ads on YouTube In-Feed, YouTube Shorts, Gmail, and Google Discover — mid-funnel, social-style placements. Launched as Discovery Ads in 2019, renamed Demand Gen in 2023 with expanded video formats.
Key differences from Display: Demand Gen uses Google's curated premium placements (YouTube, Gmail, Discover) not the open Display Network. Higher quality placements, more engaging formats, better for consideration campaigns.
Targeting: Interest segments, Custom Intent (people who searched certain keywords), Similar audiences, Customer Match.
Best for: Product launches, mid-funnel nurturing, reaching users similar to your best customers.
Bidding: Maximize Conversions, Target CPA, or Target ROAS.

🇮🇳 India Example: Meesho uses Demand Gen to show their fashion product videos on YouTube In-Feed and Discover to users who searched for 'affordable ethnic wear online' and 'Meesho alternatives.' Demand Gen CPL is 40% higher than Search but reaches 3x more people — essential for growing from awareness to purchase consideration.

Q46. What is Google Ads A/B testing and how do you run it properly?
Advanced▾

Google Ads A/B testing (called Experiments in Google Ads) compares two versions of a campaign element to determine which performs better at statistical significance.
Types of experiments:
1. Ad variation experiments — test different headlines, descriptions
2. Campaign experiments — test bidding strategy changes, landing pages
3. Search custom experiments — test changes to keywords, match types
Proper A/B test principles:
1. Change ONE variable at a time
2. Run for minimum 2 weeks, ideally 4 weeks
3. Need sufficient data — aim for 100+ conversions per variant
4. Use 50/50 traffic split (or 70/30 if risk averse)
5. Check statistical significance before declaring winner (aim for 95%+)
6. Never end test early just because one variant looks better — statistical fluctuation is common in week 1

🇮🇳 India Example: Naukri.com runs an ad copy A/B test: Version A headline: 'Upload Resume — Get Job Alerts' vs Version B: 'Recruiters View Your Profile Daily.' After 3 weeks and 480 conversions across both variants (240 each), Version B shows 28% higher conversion rate at 96% statistical significance. They roll out Version B across all campaigns. The key: they waited 3 weeks (not 3 days), ensured equal traffic split, and only changed the headline — not headline + description + landing page simultaneously.

Q48. How do you handle a Google Ads account that has been disapproved or suspended?
Advanced▾

Google Ads disapprovals and suspensions require different responses:
Ad disapprovals: Single ads violate policies. Fix the ad (remove disallowed content) or appeal. Common reasons in India: misleading price claims, unauthorized pharmacy, financial products without disclaimers.
Account suspensions: Entire account disabled. More serious. Common reasons:
1. Circumventing systems (VPN, hiding landing page from Google)
2. Billing issues (failed payments, billing location mismatch)
3. Policy violations at account level (dangerous products, impersonation)
Resolution process:
1. Read the exact policy violation cited
2. Fix all pages, ads and targeting that violate policy
3. Submit appeal with specific explanation of what was fixed
4. Wait 2–5 business days
5. If denied, escalate to Google Ads support directly
Prevention: Regular policy audits, especially when adding new services or markets.

🇮🇳 India Example: A real estate client's account suspended for 'circumventing systems' — their landing page had a redirect that showed a different page to Google's bot than to real users (done by their developer to hide low stock). Fix: Remove the redirect, show same content to all visitors, update all landing pages. Appeal with explanation and screenshots. Account reinstated in 4 days. Post-suspension, run all new landing pages through Google Ads Preview Tool to verify what Google sees before running campaigns.
Q49. What is cross-channel attribution and how does Google Ads fit into the full marketing funnel?
Advanced▾

Cross-channel attribution assigns credit to different marketing touchpoints across the full customer journey — not just the last click before conversion.
Full funnel example for a digital product:
Day 1: User sees YouTube ad (awareness) → Day 5: Clicks Meta ad (consideration) → Day 10: Searches Google, clicks Search ad (conversion). Last-click gives 100% to Search. Data-driven gives 20% YouTube, 35% Meta, 45% Search.
Why this matters for Google Ads budget: If you only measure last-click conversions, you'll over-invest in Search (last touch) and under-invest in YouTube/Display (upper funnel) — causing Search to eventually underperform as your awareness funnel dries up.
Tools for cross-channel: GA4 attribution models, Google Ads attribution report, Looker Studio cross-channel dashboards, third-party tools (Northbeam, Triple Whale).

🇮🇳 India Example: Swiggy's typical customer journey: Instagram video ad (awareness of new restaurant) → Swiggy Search ad for 'biryani delivery near me' (conversion). Instagram gets zero credit under last-click but drives 60% of new user awareness. When Swiggy reduced Meta budget by 40% to shift to Search, Search conversions dropped 25% two months later — the upper funnel dried up. Cross-channel attribution showed this connection. Lesson: Google Ads Search performance depends on upper-funnel channels you might not credit.
