50 SEO Interview Questions & Answers for 2026

Written by Neeraj Singh Bora — Digital Marketing Manager with 11+ years in Technical SEO, Google Ads & Meta Ads

🟢 Beginner — SEO Fundamentals

Q1. What is SEO and why is it important for businesses?
Beginner▾

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the process of optimizing a website to rank higher in organic (non-paid) search results. It matters because organic search is the largest source of website traffic — more cost-effective than paid ads in the long run.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Naukri.com ranks organically for thousands of job-related keywords like 'jobs in Mumbai' or 'digital marketing jobs Delhi.' This organic traffic is worth crores in ad spend. Without SEO, they would need to pay Google Ads for every visitor.
Q2. What are the 3 main pillars of SEO?
Beginner▾

1. Technical SEO — Website structure, crawlability, speed, mobile-friendliness, Core Web Vitals.
2. On-Page SEO — Content quality, keyword optimization, meta tags, headings, internal linking.
3. Off-Page SEO — Backlinks, brand mentions, E-E-A-T signals, social signals.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Zomato's SEO success is built on all three: Technical (fast-loading restaurant pages), On-Page (location + cuisine keywords), Off-Page (backlinks from food magazines, press coverage). Remove any pillar and rankings drop.
Q3. What is the difference between On-Page and Off-Page SEO?
Beginner▾

On-Page SEO = everything you control on your own website: title tags, meta descriptions, H1-H6 headings, content quality, keyword placement, image alt text, internal links, URL structure, page speed.
Off-Page SEO = signals coming from outside your website: backlinks from other sites, brand mentions, Google Business Profile, social media signals, PR coverage.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: PolicyBazaar's On-Page SEO includes optimized title tags for 'best health insurance plan India.' Their Off-Page SEO includes backlinks from Economic Times, Mint, and financial blogs — signaling to Google that they are an authoritative financial resource.
Q4. What is a backlink and why do quality backlinks matter more than quantity?
Beginner▾

A backlink is a link from another website pointing to your website. It acts as a 'vote of confidence' — Google treats quality backlinks as endorsements of your content's credibility and authority.
Quality over quantity: One backlink from Forbes.com is worth more than 1,000 links from low-authority spam sites. Key factors: Domain Authority, relevance to your topic, placement (editorial vs footer), anchor text.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Byju's getting a backlink from IIT's official website is extremely valuable — highly relevant, high authority, editorial. Byju's buying 500 links from random directories would actually hurt rankings due to Google's spam detection algorithms.
Q5. What is the Google Search algorithm and what are its key ranking factors?
Beginner▾

Google's algorithm is a complex system using 200+ factors to rank web pages. The core factors in 2026:
1. Content quality and relevance — Does the page fully answer the search query?
2. E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness
3. Page Experience — Core Web Vitals, mobile-friendliness, HTTPS
4. Backlinks — Quality and quantity of inbound links
5. User signals — Click-through rate, dwell time, bounce rate

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: When you search 'income tax filing India,' the top results are Income Tax India government site (authority), ClearTax (E-E-A-T — CA experts write content), and Zerodha Varsity (quality content). All three score high on multiple ranking factors.

🟡 Intermediate — Technical & On-Page

Q6. What are Core Web Vitals and why does Google care about them?
Intermediate▾

Core Web Vitals are Google's three page experience metrics:
LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) — Loading speed. Target: under 2.5 seconds
INP (Interaction to Next Paint) — Responsiveness. Target: under 200ms
CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) — Visual stability. Target: under 0.1
Google uses these as ranking signals because they measure actual user experience quality.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: A Myntra product page with LCP of 6 seconds loses customers and rankings. After optimization (image compression, CDN, lazy loading), LCP drops to 1.8 seconds — better user experience and higher Google ranking for competitive product keywords.
Q7. What is E-E-A-T and why did Google add the extra 'E'?
Intermediate▾

E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Google added 'Experience' in December 2022 to reward content from people with real, first-hand experience on a topic — not just research-based expertise.
Why it matters: For YMYL (Your Money Your Life) topics — health, finance, legal — Google heavily weighs E-E-A-T. A CA who actually files taxes carries more weight than a random blog writer.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: A blog post about 'GST filing process' written by a CA with their credentials visible (photo, qualifications, firm name) ranks higher than the same content on an anonymous website. Neeraj Singh Bora writing about digital marketing SEO — 11 years experience — is a strong E-E-A-T signal.
Q8. What is schema markup and how does it improve SEO?
Intermediate▾

Schema markup is structured data code (JSON-LD format) added to web pages to help search engines understand content context. It enables Rich Results in Google SERPs — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, recipe cards, event dates.
Most important schemas in 2026: FAQPage, Article, Product, LocalBusiness, HowTo, BreadcrumbList, Person

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Swiggy adds Schema markup (Restaurant, MenuSection, AggregateRating) to every restaurant page. Google then shows star ratings and cuisine type directly in search results — increasing CTR by 20-30% without any ranking change.
Q9. What is canonical tag and when should you use it?
Intermediate▾

A canonical tag tells Google which version of a page is the 'master' version when duplicate or similar content exists across multiple URLs. It prevents duplicate content issues and consolidates link equity.

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Flipkart has the same product available at multiple URLs — /product?id=123, /electronics/phone/product, /offers/product. The canonical tag on all variants points to the primary URL, ensuring Google indexes only one version and consolidates all backlinks to that single page.
Q10. What is keyword cannibalization and how do you fix it?
Intermediate▾

Keyword cannibalization occurs when multiple pages on your website compete for the same keyword, splitting authority and confusing Google about which page to rank.
Fix strategies: Merge similar pages, set canonical tags, use 301 redirects, differentiate content focus, add internal links pointing to the page you want to rank

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Naukri.com had 3 pages all targeting 'jobs in Delhi': /delhi-jobs, /jobs?location=delhi, /career/delhi. Cannibalization split their authority. Solution: redirect /jobs?location=delhi and /career/delhi to /delhi-jobs with 301 redirects, consolidating all authority to one URL.

🔴 Advanced — 2026 & GEO Strategy

Q11. What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) and how does it differ from SEO?
Advanced▾

SEO = optimizing to rank in Google's blue link results.
GEO = optimizing to be cited or summarized by AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini).
Key differences:
SEO targets keywords → GEO targets entities and topics
SEO builds backlinks → GEO builds citations and brand mentions
SEO measures rankings → GEO measures AI mentions and citation frequency

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: When someone asks ChatGPT 'best CA firm for startup tax in India,' and CAOnWeb.com appears as a cited source — that's GEO working. The factors that led to this: authoritative content, structured FAQ schema, E-E-A-T signals, and entity optimization (CAOnWeb as a known entity for CA services).
Q12. What content structure works best for ranking in both Google and AI search in 2026?
Advanced▾

The 2026 content structure for Google + LLM ranking:
1. Direct Answer First — Answer the question in the first 2-3 sentences (for featured snippets + AI extraction)
2. Structured headings — H2/H3 that match question format
3. FAQ Section — with FAQPage schema (directly fed into Google AI Overviews)
4. Author Credentials — Name, experience, certifications (E-E-A-T signal)
5. Statistical Evidence — Numbers and data that AI models cite
6. Internal entity linking — Link to related concepts on your site

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: NerdWallet India creates content with direct answers, clear author credentials (CFP certified advisors), FAQ schemas, and cited statistics. Result: Google AI Overviews cite their content in 40% of financial queries. The same content ranks #1 organically AND appears in AI answers — double traffic from one well-structured page.
Q13. What is zero-click search and how should it change your SEO strategy?
Advanced▾

Zero-click search occurs when Google answers the query directly in the SERP — featured snippets, Knowledge Panels, Google AI Overviews — so the user never clicks any result. In India, zero-click is estimated at 60%+ of all searches in 2026.
Strategy shift:
Stop optimizing only for rankings → optimize for featured snippet extraction
Add direct-answer Q&A format → Google shows your content AS the answer
Focus on brand queries → zero-click builds brand awareness even without clicks
Use schema markup → increases chance of rich result extraction

🇮🇳 India Example: India Example: Someone searches 'GST rate for software services India.' Google AI Overview shows the answer directly — 18%. CAOnWeb gets zero clicks from this query. Their new strategy: optimize for brand queries ('CAOnWeb GST services') where zero-click still shows their brand, plus capture zero-click visibility as a credibility signal for prospective clients who DO visit.
